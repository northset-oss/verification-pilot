# Northset OSS Run Records — Payment Policy

Most of what we offer costs a maintainer nothing and involves no money at all. This page is the
rules for the cases where money is on the table. They exist to protect your independence, not our
budget.

## Right now: we are not moving money

During this pilot we are not moving money at all — we don't send payments, offer honoraria, or
fund bounties. The rules below are what will apply if that changes, and we'll update this page
before any money moves.

## The rules (when payment applies)

1. **Nothing we pay is ever tied to a merge.** No payment depends on you merging, approving, or
   deciding in our favor. Our records hard-code this, and our own tooling rejects any record that
   marks a payment as merge-contingent. Your review is yours; our money never rides on the
   outcome.
2. **When we do pay, we prefer the least entangling option, in this order:**
   - A donation to the project through the funding option it already uses.
   - A one-time GitHub Sponsors payment from the organization — which discloses to you that the
     money comes from a business. We consider that disclosure a feature, not a cost.
   - A fixed amount for review time (roughly $25–100), agreed in writing in advance and paid the
     same whether the outcome is a merge, a rejection, or silence.
   - A one-off through a project's public collective, where the ledger is open by default.
3. **We match the funding option you already have.** We won't ask you to set up a new way to
   receive money.
4. **We never claim a bounty we funded.** Putting up money and then collecting it ourselves would
   be dishonest — so we don't, from any account, ever.
5. **We only fund a bounty where the invitation is built in** — the project already runs a public
   bounty board, or the maintainer set one up, or the issue itself asks for funding. Never a cold
   "here's a bounty" comment.
6. **Every payment is on the record** — the run record notes whether a payment was made and that
   it was not tied to a merge. (It records the fact of payment, not your bank details.)
